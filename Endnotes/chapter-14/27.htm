What is the Castle and Moat Security Model? (https://www.keepersecurity.com/resources/glossary/what-is-the-castle-and-moat-security-model)

"Smart castle builders in the medieval ages knew the value of layered security and created multiple hurdles to challenge attackers. Deep moats, often rumored to be filled with alligators, prevented invaders from tunneling under the castle. Towering double walls withstood the hurl of battering rams and catapults. High walk walls were used to sling arrows and other direct attack methods to prevent breaches into the castle's core." - Source E.O. Johnson; 2023 - Source: Layered Security: Think Castle Walls and a Moat Full of Alligators (Castle Defense); 2024

As with a castle, layered security makes it possible for you to protect sensitive core data with protective surrounding walls. Layered security may look like:

1.      A perpetrator impersonates a CEO by issuing an email with questions asking employees to click on a malicious link or attachment.

2.      In the first layer of defense an anti-spam solution detects the email and preventing its ever appearing in your email inbox.

3.      A second layer is activated if your anti-spam software doesn't catch the malicious activity and your local workstations virus protection software quarantines the malicious software the email tried to activate.

4.    Then a third layer of defense should protect against activity like a Zero-Day attack, but there is no known remedy for this yet.

The malicious actor has now wreaked havoc out your enterprise data; this activity results in a Zero-Day Exploit, or Zero-Day Attack.

Only an Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) / Intrusion Prevention System (IPS) from companies like Juniper can resolve the active exploit attack by heuristic analysis of the vector.

Zero Trust by the Numbers

1.      80% of data breaches involve compromised privileged credentials (Forrester)

2.      In 2020 alone, there were over 1,000 known data breaches in the United States, affecting over 150 million individuals (Statista)

3.      72% of organizations planned to implement Zero Trust in 2020 (IntelligentCISO)

4.      By 2022, 80% of new business applications 2020 opened up to ecosystem partners will be accessed through zero-trust network access (Gartner)

5.      By 2023, 60% of enterprises will phase out most of their remote access virtual private networks (VPNs) in favour of Zero Trust Network Access (Gartner)

6.      40% of cyber breaches actually originate with authorized users accessing unauthorized systems (IDC)

7.      By 2025, more than 85% of successful attacks against modern enterprise user endpoints will exploit configuration and user errors rather than make use of advanced malware (Gartner)
